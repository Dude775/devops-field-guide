

Hands-On 7.6 conclusion

Created:
- dev Namespace
- dev-quota ResourceQuota
- color-api-deployment in dev namespace

Initial Deployment:
- replicas: 4
- image: lironefitoussi/color-api:1.0.0
- each Pod used:
  - requests.cpu: 200m
  - requests.memory: 256Mi
  - limits.cpu: 500m
  - limits.memory: 512Mi

Initial quota usage:
- limits.cpu reached 2 / 2
- limits.memory reached 2Gi / 2Gi
- requests.memory reached 1Gi / 1Gi

Rolling Update:
- image changed to lironefitoussi/color-api:1.1.0
- rollout got stuck because Kubernetes needed temporary extra Pods
- ReplicaSet showed FailedCreate events with exceeded quota

Fix:
- dev-quota was expanded:
  - requests.cpu: 2
  - requests.memory: 2Gi
  - limits.cpu: 4
  - limits.memory: 4Gi
- rollout restart completed successfully after quota expansion

Scaling:
- scaling to 10 replicas was attempted
- Kubernetes again blocked new Pod creation because quota was fully used
- ReplicaSet events showed exceeded quota

Important note:
The original 1.0.0 image showed ImagePullBackOff in this environment, but the quota behavior was still visible and correctly proven through ReplicaSet events.

Conclusion:
ResourceQuota affects every new Pod creation, including Rolling Updates and Scaling.
The real failure is usually visible in ReplicaSet events, not only in kubectl apply output.
