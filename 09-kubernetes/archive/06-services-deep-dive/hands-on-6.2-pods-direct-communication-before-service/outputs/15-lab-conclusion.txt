

Hands-On 6.2 conclusion

Traffic generator was configured to send requests directly to one Pod IP.
After deleting that target Pod, the Deployment created a replacement Pod with a new IP.
The traffic-generator Pod kept using the old IP from traffic-generator.yaml.

Result:
- Deployment recovered the missing replica.
- Client did not discover the new Pod IP.
- Direct Pod-to-Pod communication by Pod IP is unstable.
- This is why Kubernetes needs a Service abstraction, usually ClusterIP.

Observed behavior:
Before delete: logs included COLOR and HOSTNAME.
After delete: logs showed empty timestamp lines without COLOR/HOSTNAME.
